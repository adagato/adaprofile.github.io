Graphics

Introduction

I have been tasked with the creation of graphics many times as a part of both my education and work experience. In doing so, I have become proficient in Adobe InDesign as well as the GIMP photo editing platform. Additionally, I have developed an understanding for the efficiency and effectiveness of using graphics in communication. Many of my graphics incorporate Gestalt theory, where viewers are intended to view the document as a whole instead of as a collection of the individual parts. To accomplish this, you'll find that every element on the graphics presented here is there for a reason, even if that reason is to blend into the background of a larger scene.

  1. Artemis Launch

    2. As a part of my job on Missouri S&T's IT Relations team, I frequently worked with groups outside the IT department to create communications material for them. One of the campus's means of communication is the MinerBytes system, which is a series of televisions and monitors across campus that display information in the form of graphics or short animations. I was contacted to create such a graphic for a group on campus that wanted to host a watch event for NASA's Artemis I launch in the fall of 2022. As the MinerBytes screens can be oriented both vertically or horizontally, I needed to create a design for both orientations. Both representations of the design are shown here.






  2. Southwinds Submissions Flyer

    3. In my role on the Southwinds literary arts magazine club, I was tasked with spreading the word that the club was gathering submissions for our next issue. Part of this campaign was a flyer design that was distributed across buildings on campus, which called for submissions and provided a QR code link to the (now closed) form that would allow students to submit their work from their mobile devices.
