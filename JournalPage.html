Computers and Composition Online Review

What is Computers and Composition Online?

Computers and Composition Online is a journal focused on the interactions between technology and the classroom. The journal lists five major sections that its submissions should align with:

  1. Theory into Practice

    2. This section includes long, research-based pieces focused on rhetorical and critical theories surrounding technology, while making note of the implications of this technology for teaching.

  2. The Virtual Classroom

    3. This section asks for materials derived from teaching experience, including syllawebs, resource sites, and student projects.

  3. Professional Development

    4. This section discusses the past, present, and future of Computers and Composition Online through interviews, conferences,and calls for submissions.

  4. Print to Screen

    5. This section includes online features that are relevant to current print journal themes as well as special topics.

  5. Reviews

    6. Included in this section are reviews of books, sites, apps, events, and other blended media. It can also include suggestions of items to review.



Common Questions

  1. How frequently does Computers and Composition Online publish?

    2. Rather than publishing in scheduled editions, Computers and Composition Online publishes as soon as a submission has been fully accepted. This comes as a result of the journal being entirely online, with no print publications.

  2. How strict is the submission process?

    3. The journal states that it has rather lenient submission guidelines, with language that reads "If you have work available, we encourage you to submit." That said, all submissions must be intended for the web, not print. More information about formatting conventions can be found below.

  3. What is the peer review process? How long does it take?

    4. Submissions are screened by staff for content relevancy and formatting before being sent to the review board for an external peer review. The process takes roughly 4 weeks during the academic year. In some cases, submissions may be sent to a C&W specialist to ensure that it is reviewed by current subject experts.

  4. Is the journal focused on academics or industry?

    5. This journal focuses heavily on academics, especially the interactions between academics and technology.

  5. What organization owns or manages this journal?

    6. Computers and Composition Online is the online-only "companion journal" to Computers and Composition: An International Journal. It is not connected to any conferences. The journal does have awards, however, with five awards being handed out in different categories. Nominations for this year's awards are due on March 15th.

  6. How relevant was this journal to Online Social Movements?

    7. While being a journal focused primarily on academia, it still contains a wide range of articles that can fit the subject matter of the Online Social Movements class. Particularly relevant are discussions of how technology and technological movements are changing the nature of classrooms and the social structures found there. In particular, there is a variety of papers discussing technofeminism, many of which can be connected with the material found in our class. All of the papers I found were linked from the journal's homepage.

  7. What format does this journal require?

    8. Computers and Composition Online, with its heavy focus on the usage of technology in the classroom, asks that submissions make use of the tools available to them in an online format. The journal only accepts online submissions in order to further encourage this type of submission style. Common features of journal submissions include a small website-like layout, with a page heading, navlinks, and other multimedia functions. Submissions can include videos, audio recordings, images, and other software pieces. That said, all video and audio files must be captioned or come with transcripts, alt text should be included, and iamges must be original works or used with permission. For examples of submissions that make use of these web-specific features, see the list of submissions featured on the journal's homepage.

    Note: For citations, APA style is preferred.
